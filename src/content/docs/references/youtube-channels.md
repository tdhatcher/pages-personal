The channels I actually keep up with.

## .NET & C#

- [**Nick Chapsas**](https://www.youtube.com/@nickchapsas) — Deep dives on modern .NET patterns, performance, and best practices. Consistently high quality.
- [**Tim Corey**](https://www.youtube.com/@IAmTimCorey) — Practical .NET from beginner to advanced. Great for filling gaps.
- [**raw coding**](https://www.youtube.com/@RawCoding) — ASP.NET Core internals, auth, middleware. Goes deeper than most.
- [**Zoran Horvat**](https://www.youtube.com/@zoran-horvat) — Functional patterns and clean code in C#. Makes you think differently.

## AI & ML

- [**Andrej Karpathy**](https://www.youtube.com/@AndrejKarpathy) — Bottom-up explanations of how LLMs actually work. Essential viewing.
- [**Matt Wolfe**](https://www.youtube.com/@mreflow) — Weekly roundup of AI tools and news. Great for staying current.
- [**AI Explained**](https://www.youtube.com/@aiexplained-official) — Solid breakdowns of new model releases and research papers.

## General Software Engineering

- [**Theo - t3.gg**](https://www.youtube.com/@t3dotgg) — Opinionated takes on web dev and the broader industry. Entertaining and often right.
- [**Fireship**](https://www.youtube.com/@Fireship) — 100-second explainers and longer tutorials. Best production quality on YouTube.
- [**ThePrimeagen**](https://www.youtube.com/@ThePrimeagen) — Vim, performance, engineering culture. Chaotic but genuinely educational.
